New Zealand's Biodiversity

Discover the incredible variety of native species that call Aotearoa home

New Zealand's isolation for millions of years has created a unique ecosystem with many endemic species found nowhere else on Earth. From ancient kauri forests to alpine herbs, from flightless birds to marine mammals, our biodiversity is both precious and fragile.

About Aotearoa Native Database

Our mission to protect and promote New Zealand's native biodiversity

Our Mission

The Aotearoa Native Database is dedicated to protecting and promoting New Zealand's unique native biodiversity through education, awareness, and community engagement. We believe that knowledge is the first step toward conservation, and by connecting people with our native species, we can inspire action to protect them for future generations.

Our Goals

  • Educate New Zealanders and visitors about our native species
  • Raise awareness about conservation challenges and successes
  • Support community-led conservation initiatives
  • Promote sustainable practices that protect native habitats
  • Honor M캐ori traditional knowledge and guardianship (kaitiakitanga)

Educational Mission

Education is at the heart of everything we do. Our platform provides accessible, accurate information about New Zealand's native species, their habitats, threats they face, and conservation efforts underway. We aim to make this knowledge available to everyone, from school children to researchers, tourists to lifelong residents.
